Applications: The Normal Line

While tangents skim the surface of a curve, Normals pierce directly through it. A normal line is perfectly perpendicular (at exactly $90^\circ$) to the tangent line at the point of contact. In physics, normals are vital for calculating forces, reflections, and structural stress.

The Negative Reciprocal Rule

Because the normal is perpendicular to the tangent, their gradients are mathematically linked. If the gradient of the tangent is $m_T$, and the gradient of the normal is $m_N$, their relationship is always:

$$m_T \times m_N = -1 \quad \text{or} \quad m_N = -\frac{1}{m_T}$$

Standard Recipe for a Normal Line:
  1. Differentiate: Find $\frac{dy}{dx}$ to get the gradient formula.
  2. Find Tangent Gradient ($m_T$): Substitute your $x$-value to find the steepness of the curve.
  3. Flip & Change Sign ($m_N$): Flip the fraction of $m_T$ and multiply by $-1$. (e.g., if $m_T = 2$, then $m_N = -\frac{1}{2}$).
  4. Construct: Use your point and your new normal gradient in the equation $y - y_1 = m_N(x - x_1)$.

Worked Solutions

🟢 Easy: Basic Normal

Find the equation of the normal to $y = x^2 - 3x$ at the point where $x = 2$.

  • Find the Point: Plug $x = 2$ into the original function. $y = (2)^2 - 3(2) = 4 - 6 = -2$. The point is $(2, -2)$.
  • Differentiate: Using the power rule, $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2x - 3$.
  • Find Tangent Gradient ($m_T$): At $x = 2$, $m_T = 2(2) - 3 = 1$.
  • Find Normal Gradient ($m_N$): The negative reciprocal of $1$ is $-\frac{1}{1}$, which is $-1$. So, $m_N = -1$.
  • Construct Line: $y - (-2) = -1(x - 2) \implies y + 2 = -x + 2 \implies y = -x$.

🟡 Medium: Trigonometric Normal

Find the equation of the normal to $y = \cos(x)$ at $x = \frac{\pi}{2}$.

  • Find the Point: $y = \cos(\frac{\pi}{2}) = 0$. Point is $(\frac{\pi}{2}, 0)$.
  • Differentiate: The derivative of $\cos(x)$ is $-\sin(x)$. So $\frac{dy}{dx} = -\sin(x)$.
  • Find Tangent Gradient ($m_T$): Substitute $x = \frac{\pi}{2}$. $m_T = -\sin(\frac{\pi}{2}) = -1$.
  • Find Normal Gradient ($m_N$): Flip and change the sign of $-1$. $m_N = -\frac{1}{-1} = 1$.
  • Construct Line: $y - 0 = 1(x - \frac{\pi}{2}) \implies y = x - \frac{\pi}{2}$.

🔴 Hard: Logarithmic Challenge

Find the equation of the normal to the curve $y = \ln(x)$ at $x = e$.

  • Find the Point: $y = \ln(e)$. Since $\ln$ is $\log_e$, $\ln(e) = 1$. Point is $(e, 1)$.
  • Differentiate: The derivative of $\ln(x)$ is a standard result: $\frac{dy}{dx} = \frac{1}{x}$.
  • Find Tangent Gradient ($m_T$): Substitute $x = e$. $m_T = \frac{1}{e}$.
  • Find Normal Gradient ($m_N$): The negative reciprocal of $\frac{1}{e}$ is $-e$. So, $m_N = -e$.
  • Construct Line: $y - 1 = -e(x - e)$. Expanding gives $y - 1 = -ex + e^2 \implies y = -ex + e^2 + 1$. Leave it exact!
