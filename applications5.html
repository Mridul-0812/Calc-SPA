Harder Motion & Graphing

Mastering the physical interpretation of derivatives through visual modelling.

The Calculus Hierarchy

In kinematics, everything is linked by the derivative. If you have a function for where something is, you can find its speed and its acceleration just by differentiating.

1. Displacement $x(t)$
The position of an object relative to an origin at time $t$.

2. Velocity $v(t) = \frac{dx}{dt}$
The rate of change of displacement. If $v(t) = 0$, the object is instantaneously at rest.

3. Acceleration $a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = \frac{d^2x}{dt^2}$
The rate at which velocity changes. If $a(t) = 0$, velocity is constant (a stationary point on the velocity graph!).

Pro Tip: The Graph Secret

  • The Gradient of a Displacement graph is Velocity.
  • The Gradient of a Velocity graph is Acceleration.
  • A Stationary Point on the Displacement graph means the object has stopped and is likely changing direction.

Visualizing Stationary Points

Before we sketch, we must understand what "Turning Points" actually mean in the real world. A turning point occurs when the gradient is zero ($f'(x) = 0$).

Local Maximum

The object reaches its furthest positive point before turning back.

Local Minimum

The object reaches its furthest negative point before turning back.

Point of Inflection

The object pauses momentarily but continues in the same direction.

Worked Solutions: Drawing Trends

1. The Quadratic Launch

A ball is thrown with $x(t) = 4t - t^2$. Find $v(t)$ and describe the motion.

Solution: Differentiating gives $v(t) = 4 - 2t$.
At $t=0, v=4$. At $t=2, v=0$ (at rest/max height).
The velocity graph is a straight line starting at 4 and crossing the axis at $t=2$.

2. Cubic Oscillations

A particle moves with $x(t) = t^3 - 6t^2 + 9t$. When does it change direction?

Solution: $v(t) = 3t^2 - 12t + 9 = 3(t-1)(t-3)$.
It stops at $t=1$ and $t=3$.
Graphically, the velocity is a parabola hitting zero at 1 and 3.

3. Exponential Decay

A car brakes with $x(t) = 10(1 - e^{-t})$. Find the velocity after a long time.

Solution: $v(t) = 10e^{-t}$.
As $t \to \infty, v(t) \to 0$.
The velocity graph starts high and approaches the axis as an asymptote.
