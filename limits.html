The Foundation: Limits

Before we can calculate the exact rate of change at a specific moment, we need to understand what happens when we get infinitely close to that moment. Welcome to the concept of the Limit.

What is a Limit?

In mathematics, a limit tells us the value that a function approaches as the input ($x$) gets closer and closer to some number. The actual value of the function exactly *at* that number might be broken (undefined), but the limit still exists!

We write this in proper mathematical notation as:

$$\lim_{x \to a} f(x) = L$$

This reads as: "The limit of $f(x)$ as $x$ approaches $a$, is equal to $L$."

How to Evaluate Limits: The 3 Steps
  • Step 1: Direct Substitution. Just plug the number in! If $\lim_{x \to 3} (2x + 1)$, just do $2(3) + 1 = 7$. You're done.
  • Step 2: Check for $0/0$. If you substitute and get $\frac{0}{0}$, this is called an indeterminate form. It means there is a "hole" in the graph, but a limit still exists hiding behind the math!
  • Step 3: Factor & Cancel. If you get $0/0$, factorise the numerator and denominator algebraically. Cancel out the matching brackets, and then try substituting again.

Worked Solutions

🟢 Easy: Direct Sub

Evaluate $$\lim_{x \to 5} (x^2 - 3x)$$

  • Substitute $x = 5$: Plug it straight in.
    $(5)^2 - 3(5)$
  • Calculate: $25 - 15 = 10$.
  • Result: Since we didn't get $0/0$, the limit is just $10$.

🟡 Medium: The 0/0 Hole

Evaluate $$\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{x^2 - 9}{x - 3}$$

  • Substitute: $\frac{3^2 - 9}{3 - 3} = \frac{0}{0}$. We must factorise!
  • Factorise top: Difference of two squares.
    $\lim_{x \to 3} \frac{(x - 3)(x + 3)}{x - 3}$
  • Cancel: Cross out $(x-3)$. We are left with $\lim_{x \to 3} (x + 3)$.
  • Substitute again: $3 + 3 = 6$. The limit is $6$.

🔴 Hard: Harder Quadratics

Evaluate $$\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{x^2 + x - 6}{x - 2}$$

  • Check: Subbing in $x=2$ gives $\frac{4 + 2 - 6}{0} = \frac{0}{0}$.
  • Factorise top: What multiplies to $-6$ and adds to $1$? ($+3$ and $-2$).
    $\lim_{x \to 2} \frac{(x + 3)(x - 2)}{x - 2}$
  • Cancel & Sub: Cancel the $(x-2)$.
    $\lim_{x \to 2} (x + 3) = 2 + 3 = 5$.

👑 Mastery: Expansion

Evaluate $$\lim_{h \to 0} \frac{(x + h)^2 - x^2}{h}$$

  • Note: We are treating $x$ as a constant and taking the limit as $h$ goes to $0$. Subbing $h=0$ gives $0/0$.
  • Expand the top: $(x^2 + 2xh + h^2) - x^2$
  • Simplify: The $x^2$ terms cancel, leaving $\frac{2xh + h^2}{h}$.
  • Factorise $h$: $\frac{h(2x + h)}{h} = 2x + h$.
  • Substitute $h=0$: $2x + 0 = 2x$. (This is a sneak peek at First Principles!)
