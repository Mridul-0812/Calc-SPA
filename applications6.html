Horizontal Dynamics

Unpacking the relationship between vectors, direction, and speed.

The "Sign" Misconception

In kinematics, many students believe that a negative acceleration automatically means an object is slowing down. This is a mathematical trap!

Acceleration is a vector. The sign $(+/-)$ only tells you the direction of the push. To know if an object is actually getting faster, you must compare that push to the direction it is currently moving (Velocity).

The Golden Rule of Speed

Speed is the magnitude of velocity ($|v|$). An object's speed increases if the acceleration "helps" the velocity, and decreases if it "fights" it.

Speeding Up

$v$ and $a$ have the SAME SIGN

$v > 0, a > 0$ (Rightward push)
$v < 0, a < 0$ (Leftward push)

$v \times a > 0$

Slowing Down

$v$ and $a$ have OPPOSITE SIGNS

$v > 0, a < 0$ (Braking to left)
$v < 0, a > 0$ (Braking to right)

$v \times a < 0$

Worked Solutions

Solution 1: Determining State

A particle moves with displacement $x = t^2 - 10t + 5$. Is it speeding up at $t = 2$?

1. Find Velocity: $v(t) = \frac{dx}{dt} = 2t - 10$

2. Find Acceleration: $a(t) = \frac{dv}{dt} = 2$

3. Evaluate at $t=2$:
$v(2) = 2(2) - 10 = -6$
$a(2) = 2$ (always positive)

Conclusion: Since $v$ is negative and $a$ is positive (opposite signs), the particle is Slowing Down.

Solution 2: Finding the Domain

For what values of $t$ is the particle $x = \frac{1}{3}t^3 - 4t^2 + 12t$ speeding up for $t \ge 0$?

Step 1: $v(t) = t^2 - 8t + 12 = (t-2)(t-6)$
$v > 0$ on $(0,2)$ and $(6, \infty)$; $v < 0$ on $(2,6)$.

Step 2: $a(t) = 2t - 8 = 2(t-4)$
$a > 0$ for $t > 4$; $a < 0$ for $t < 4$.

Step 3: Match Signs:
- Speeding up when both are positive: $t > 6$.
- Speeding up when both are negative: $2 < t < 4$.

Final Domain: $2 < t < 4$ or $t > 6$.
