Three Worked Solutions

Easy: Linear

Find the derivative of $f(x) = 7x$ from first principles.

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{7(x+h) - 7x}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{7x + 7h - 7x}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{7h}{h} = 7$$

Result: $f'(x) = 7$

Medium: Quadratic

Find the derivative of $f(x) = x^2 + 2x$ from first principles.

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{((x+h)^2 + 2(x+h)) - (x^2 + 2x)}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{x^2 + 2xh + h^2 + 2x + 2h - x^2 - 2x}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{2xh + h^2 + 2h}{h} = \lim_{h \to 0} (2x + h + 2)$$

Result: $f'(x) = 2x + 2$

Hard: The Shortcut Proof

Find the derivative of $f(x) = 3x^2$ from first principles.

$$f'(x) = \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{3(x+h)^2 - 3x^2}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{3(x^2 + 2xh + h^2) - 3x^2}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} \frac{3x^2 + 6xh + 3h^2 - 3x^2}{h}$$ $$= \lim_{h \to 0} (6x + 3h)$$

Result: $f'(x) = 6x$
