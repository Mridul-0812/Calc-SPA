Real World Calculus

Differentiation isn't just about moving numbers around. It allows us to find the exact steepness of any curve at any point. Once we have that, we can draw the Tangent Line—the straight path that mimics the curve's direction at that precise moment.

Tangents & Gradients

A tangent is a straight line that "just touches" a curve at a single point. It doesn't cut through the curve like a chord; it glides past it, sharing the exact same gradient as the curve at that specific location.

Standard Recipe for a Tangent:
  1. Differentiate: Get $f'(x)$ to find the universal formula for the gradient.
  2. Get $m$: Substitute your specific $x$-value into the derivative to find the exact numerical gradient.
  3. Get $y_1$: If you are only given an $x$-coordinate, find the $y$-coordinate by plugging $x$ into the original function.
  4. Construct: You now have a gradient ($m$) and a point $(x_1, y_1)$. Use the straight-line equation: $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$.

Worked Solutions

🟢 Easy: Basic Gradient

Find the gradient of the curve $y = x^4$ at the specific point where $x = 2$.

  • Find the Derivative: First, we need the general formula for the gradient. Using the Power Rule (bring the 4 down, subtract 1 from the power), we get $\frac{dy}{dx} = 4x^3$.
  • Substitute the Value: We want the gradient specifically when $x$ is 2. Plug 2 into our derivative formula: $m = 4(2)^3$.
  • Calculate: Work out the math: $2^3 = 8$, and $4 \times 8 = 32$.
  • Final Result: The steepness (gradient) of the curve exactly at $x = 2$ is 32.

🟡 Medium: Building The Tangent

Find the full equation of the tangent line for $y = \sin(x)$ at $x = \pi$.

  • Find the Full Point: A line needs a full coordinate. Plug $x = \pi$ into the original function: $y = \sin(\pi) = 0$. Our starting point is $(\pi, 0)$.
  • Find the Gradient Formula: Differentiate the original function. The derivative of $\sin(x)$ is $\cos(x)$. So, $\frac{dy}{dx} = \cos(x)$.
  • Calculate the Exact Gradient ($m$): Plug our $x$-value into the derivative: $m = \cos(\pi) = -1$.
  • Assemble the Line: Use $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$. Substituting our values gives $y - 0 = -1(x - \pi)$. Simplifying this yields $y = -x + \pi$.

🔴 Hard: Exponential Tangent

Find the equation of the tangent to $y = e^{2x}$ at $x = 0$.

  • Find the Full Point: We need the $y$-value. Plug $x = 0$ into the original curve: $y = e^{2(0)} = e^0$. Anything to the power of 0 is 1. Our point is $(0, 1)$.
  • Differentiate (Chain Rule): The derivative of an exponential keeps the $e^{2x}$, but we must multiply by the derivative of the inside power ($2x$). This gives us $\frac{dy}{dx} = 2e^{2x}$.
  • Calculate the Exact Gradient ($m$): Substitute $x = 0$ into the derivative. $m = 2e^{2(0)} = 2 \times 1 = 2$.
  • Assemble the Line: Using $y - y_1 = m(x - x_1)$, we get $y - 1 = 2(x - 0)$. Expanding this out gives $y = 2x + 1$.

👑 Legend: The Weird Curve Intercepts

Find the $x$ and $y$-intercepts of the tangent to the curve $y = \tan(x)$ at the point $x = \frac{\pi}{4}$. Leave answers in exact form.

  • Step 1 (Coordinates): $y = \tan(\frac{\pi}{4}) = 1$. Point is $(\frac{\pi}{4}, 1)$.
  • Step 2 (Gradient): $\frac{dy}{dx} = \sec^2(x) = \frac{1}{\cos^2(x)}$.
  • Step 3 (Find $m$): $\cos(\frac{\pi}{4}) = \frac{1}{\sqrt{2}}$, so $\cos^2 = \frac{1}{2}$. Thus $m = \frac{1}{1/2} = 2$.
  • Step 4 (Equation): $y - 1 = 2(x - \frac{\pi}{4}) \implies y = 2x - \frac{\pi}{2} + 1$.
  • $y$-intercept ($x=0$): $y = 1 - \frac{\pi}{2}$.
  • $x$-intercept ($y=0$): $0 = 2x - \frac{\pi}{2} + 1 \implies x = \frac{\pi}{4} - \frac{1}{2}$.
